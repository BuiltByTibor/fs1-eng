My Journey

From Belgium to the UK - Overcoming challenges to pursue engineering

My Story

Originally from Belgium, I moved unexpectedly to the UK where I faced the challenge of learning English during sixth form. Despite starting with limited English knowledge, I achieved AAB in French, Mathematics, and Further Mathematics.

My journey wasn't easy - I was failing maths in Belgium due to lack of motivation during the pandemic and family situations. But I'm here to show you that transformation is possible with the right mindset and techniques.

Now I'm studying Mechanical Engineering at a Russell Group university, and I created this platform to share what I've learned about effective studying in STEM subjects.

My Motivation

  • To demonstrate that academic transformation is possible
  • To break down complex engineering concepts into clear explanations
  • To share organizational systems that make engineering studies manageable
  • To create a living resource that evolves with my studies

Why Flashcards for Math?

Flashcards help me remember how to approach specific problems and the methods used. In mathematics, speed is essential, and flashcards provide the repetitive practice needed to build both understanding and quick recall. By testing yourself regularly, you reinforce the neural pathways needed for fast, accurate problem-solving during exams.

Study Tools & Methods

Effective tools and methods to enhance your engineering studies

Find What Works For You

Everyone learns differently. Experiment with different study methods to find what helps you understand and retain information best.

Anki Flashcards

I use Anki for spaced repetition. It's excellent for remembering concepts, formulas, and problem-solving methods. The algorithm shows you cards just as you're about to forget them, strengthening your memory.

Microsoft Word

I summarize lectures in Word, focusing on key concepts. This forces me to process information actively rather than passively copying notes.

OneNote

Great for practice questions and diagrams. Being cloud-based means my notes are available everywhere. I organize by module > topic > subtopic.

Organization is Key

Being organized reduces stress and helps information stay in your long-term memory.

File System

I create a clear folder structure: University > Year > Module > Topic. Consistent naming helps me find materials quickly.

Makerspace

Practical work reinforces theory. Our university makerspace lets me apply concepts through hands-on projects.

Exam Preparation

I use a countdown app to plan revision. Most of my study time is spent on practice questions and flashcards for definitions.
